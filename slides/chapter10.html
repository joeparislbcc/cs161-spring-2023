More on Program Development

With tools like functions, lists, dictionaries, and other data structures in your toolbox you can do an even better job of decomposing large problems into smaller, solvable ones.

Divide-and-Conquer

The divide-and-conquer strategy is a simple one: break a large problem into smaller pieces and solve each piece individually. Then, you construct the overall solution by combining the smaller, solved parts.

Top-Down vs Bottom-Up

Top-Down Design

A top-down approach, aka stepwise design or stepwise refinement, essentially means to break down a system to gain insight into its compositional sub-systems in a reverse engineering fashion.

Bottom-Up Design

A bottom-up approach is the piecing together of systems to give rise to more complex systems, thus making the original systems sub-systems of the emergent system

In software development, the top-down and bottom-up approaches play a key role...

  • Top-down approaches emphasize planning and a complete understanding of the system.
  • It is inherent that no coding can begin until a sufficient level of detail has been reached in the design of at least some part of the system.

In software development, the top-down and bottom-up approaches play a key role...

  • Top-down approaches are implemented by attaching the stubs in place of the module.
  • This, however, delays testing of the ultimate functional units of a system until significant design is complete.

In the software development process, the top-down and bottom-up approaches play a key role...

  • Bottom-up emphasizes coding and early testing, which can begin as soon as the first module has been specified.
  • This approach, however, runs the risk that modules may be coded without having a clear idea of how they link to other parts of the system, and that such linking may not be as easy as first thought.

In the software development process, the top-down and bottom-up approaches play a key role...

  • Re-usability of code is one of the main benefits of the bottom-up approach.
